Retakes are the fastest way to sharpen decision-making under pressure. Unlike full matches, every round starts with a planted bomb and a clear job: convert or deny.

## Clear roles before you peek

On a five-man retake, assign jobs immediately: entry, trade, utility, lurk cut, and post-plant. Confusion loses more rounds than aim differentials.

## Utility is information

Smokes and flashes are not decorations. A single well-timed flash that lands as your teammate peeks is worth more than a premature swing.

## Respect wallbang lines

WallBang exists because map geometry rewards players who learn shoot-through angles. Learn common plywood, door, and box lines on Dust II, Mirage, and Inferno — then clear them intentionally.

## Trade everything

If an entry dies without a trade, the retake collapses. Hold close angles that allow immediate swing support rather than spreading across the site.

## Practice on low-latency servers

Aim transfer and counter-strafing feel different at 20ms versus 80ms. India-first servers exist so your muscle memory matches the latency you actually compete on.

## Review one habit per session

Pick a single focus — utility timing, first contact discipline, or post-plant positioning — and measure it. Retakes reward deliberate practice.
